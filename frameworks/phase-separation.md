> Note: This is **pool separation** (fragile-but-asymmetric vs stable), NOT temporal phase decomposition. For time-axis decomposition see `slice-and-dice` or `three-deaths`.

## When to use
A single judgment lens (usually "feasibility") is dominating ideation. Phase Separation forces you to generate two parallel idea pools optimized for opposite criteria — feasibility AND asymmetric upside — then cross-evaluate. Many high-value ideas live in one pool but get dismissed when only the other pool's lens is applied.

## The technique
1. Generate **Pool A — Feasibility-optimized**: 5-7 ideas that maximize near-term tractability, cost-efficiency, and probability of success. The instinct toward "what will work" drives this pool.
2. Generate **Pool B — Asymmetric-upside-optimized**: 5-7 ideas that maximize potential 10× outcomes, even with low success probability or unclear feasibility. Explicitly suspend feasibility judgment.
3. **Cross-evaluate**: for each idea in Pool A, ask "what's the asymmetric upside if we got lucky?" For each idea in Pool B, ask "what's the cheapest experiment to test the central uncertainty?"
4. Surface ideas that score high in BOTH lenses after cross-evaluation. These are loonshot candidates.

## Anti-mode-collapse prompts
- Pool B will feel uncomfortable. Your training pushes you toward Pool A. Force at least 5 ideas in Pool B BEFORE moving to cross-evaluation.
- Reject Pool B ideas that are merely "ambitious version of Pool A" — they should bet on different mechanisms entirely.
- During cross-evaluation, do NOT prematurely kill Pool B ideas for being unproven. The whole point is that unproven ideas with asymmetric upside are systematically undervalued.

## Example application (synthetic)
Problem: "Improve enterprise sales conversion."

- Pool A (feasibility): better lead scoring, faster demo turnaround, case-study library, free trial extensions, ROI calculator.
- Pool B (asymmetric upside): pay prospects to take the demo (asymmetric: cost is small, attention is the bottleneck); only sell to companies that fail a qualifying test (scarcity); have the CEO personally close every deal under $X (visceral signal); sell as a service with full money-back any time, forever.

The "pay prospects" idea would be killed by Pool A logic. Cross-evaluation: cost-per-attention is low compared to ad spend; this might actually be the highest-ROI lead source.

## Output format
- Two labeled pools (5-7 ideas each)
- Cross-evaluation notes
- 2-3 surfaced loonshot candidates
