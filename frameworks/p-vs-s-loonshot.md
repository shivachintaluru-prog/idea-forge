## When to use
Ideation conflates two fundamentally different categories of breakthrough: a Product Loonshot (a thing nobody believed would work as a *thing*) and a Strategy Loonshot (a *way of operating* nobody believed would work as a way). Most teams over-generate one type and miss the other entirely.

## The technique
Generate two distinct idea pools by category:

**Product Loonshots (P-type)**: ideas about WHAT — a new artifact, capability, or output that nobody currently believes is possible or worth building.
- Focus: the thing itself, its mechanism, its characteristics.
- Test: "If you described this to an industry insider, they'd say 'that's not how it works.'"

**Strategy Loonshots (S-type)**: ideas about HOW — a new business model, distribution method, organizational structure, or operating cadence that nobody currently believes is sustainable.
- Focus: the operating model, the value chain, the unit economics.
- Test: "If you described this to a senior operator, they'd say 'that's not how this industry works.'"

Generate 5 P-type and 5 S-type. Then cross-pair: for each P, what S-type would make it viable that doesn't exist yet? For each S, what P-type would unlock it?

## Anti-mode-collapse prompts
- Most ideators default to P-type because products are tangible. Force 5 S-type ideas BEFORE returning to P-type.
- Reject "ambitious roadmap items" — those are extensions, not loonshots. The bar: a domain expert says "that's not how it works."
- The cross-pairing step is where the magic lives. Many P-types fail because of missing S-types and vice versa; pairing surfaces both.

## Example application (synthetic)
Problem: "Reinvent enterprise software procurement."

- P-types: software that self-installs without IT involvement; software that can be uninstalled with one click and refunds prorated automatically; software that comes with embedded training videos that update with the product; software that cannot be priced — buyers set their own price post-trial.
- S-types: sales cycle <1 day, no human seller; pricing as a Dutch auction at quarter-end; the vendor pays the buyer for usage logs (data-as-currency); contracts that explicitly auto-cancel if usage drops below threshold.
- Cross-pair: "self-installing software" + "no human seller" → an entire category that bypasses traditional B2B sales motion.

## Output format
- 5 P-type ideas, 5 S-type ideas, 2-3 cross-paired hybrids
