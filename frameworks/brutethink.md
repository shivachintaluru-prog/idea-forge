## When to use
The problem feels stuck in a familiar conceptual neighborhood. Inject a deliberately unrelated stimulus and force a connection — the resulting territory is foreign by construction.

## The technique
1. Pick a RANDOM noun. Use a real source of randomness — open a book to a page, point at a word, or select from a pre-built noun list. Do NOT pick a word that "feels relevant" — that's mode collapse.
2. Without judging, list 5-10 properties or associations of the random word.
3. For each property, ask: "How does this property apply to my problem?" Generate an idea per property.
4. Keep the ideas where the connection feels surprisingly apt; discard the truly nonsensical.

The forcefulness of step 3 is the engine — when there's no obvious connection, you have to *invent* one, and the inventions are where unfamiliar ideas live.

## Anti-mode-collapse prompts
- The random word MUST be selected before you read your problem aloud — no "appropriate" word selection.
- If your first 3 connections all use the random word as a metaphor in the same way, your random word isn't doing work. Re-roll.
- The point is forced distance. If you find yourself thinking "this word doesn't apply at all," that's exactly the right starting point — apply it anyway.

## Example application (synthetic)
Problem: "Improve customer onboarding for a B2B SaaS tool."
Random word: **Lighthouse**

Properties: visible-from-far, rotates, isolated, signals-during-storms, unmanned-but-present, painted-distinctively, dangerous-shore-marker.

Forced connections:
- "Visible-from-far" → onboarding starts BEFORE signup, signaling product fit during prospect research
- "Signals-during-storms" → onboarding intensifies on user-error events, not on success states
- "Unmanned-but-present" → onboarding is a recorded-once asset, not live human handholding
- "Dangerous-shore-marker" → onboarding actively flags adjacent BAD use cases ("don't try X yet")

The 4th idea is genuinely novel and would not have surfaced from "tips for B2B onboarding."

## Output format
- The random word + its property list
- 5-10 ideas, each tagged with the property that generated it
