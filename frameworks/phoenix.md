## When to use
Apply a structured questioning checklist to dig under the problem's surface. Best when the problem feels familiar but you suspect there's depth you haven't probed.

## The technique
Answer all of the following questions about the problem. Generate at least one idea per question even when the answer feels redundant — answers compound.

**Why questions:**
- Why is this a problem worth solving?
- Why hasn't it been solved already? (What's blocking?)
- Why solve it now vs. later?

**What questions:**
- What is the problem similar to in another domain?
- What is the problem the OPPOSITE of?
- What's the smallest version of the problem? The largest?

**How questions:**
- How would you solve it if budget were 100×?
- How would you solve it if budget were 1/100×?
- How would you solve it with no technology at all?

**Who/When/Where questions:**
- Who has solved an adjacent version? What did they do differently?
- When does the problem appear and disappear?
- Where (geographically, organizationally, in the workflow) is it most acute?

## Anti-mode-collapse prompts
- Answer EVERY question, including the redundant-seeming ones. Skipping is mode collapse.
- For "what is it similar to in another domain," reach for at least one OBSCURE domain (e.g., obstetrics, geology, calligraphy) — not the closest analogue.
- For the budget questions, the constraint of 100× and 1/100× breaks linear thinking.

## Example application (synthetic)
Problem: "Reduce customer-support ticket volume."

- Why hasn't it been solved? Because each ticket is unique enough that templating fails on the long tail.
- Similar to in another domain? Triage in emergency medicine — most cases are routine, the long tail is what kills you.
- 100× budget? Ship a free 1:1 onboarding consultant for every customer.
- 1/100× budget? Ship the docs as a single 800-word page; nothing more.
- No technology? An always-on FAQ printed on the product packaging.

Each question yields 1+ idea: 12-15 raw ideas total.

## Output format
- One idea per question (12+ total)
- Tag each idea with the question that produced it
